meet ECOSAVERS

  • To learn to respect and understand animals as an essential part of our environment.
  • To foster a responsible coexistence between people and wildlife, based on empathy and care.
  • To enhance creativity and sensitivity in young people through contact with nature.
  • To create spaces where teamwork and cooperation are the drivers of change.
  • To eliminate stereotypes and promote a new culture of animal and environmental respect.

To achieve these goals, we have a team of committed volunteers and professionals who put all their knowledge, experience, and passion into ensuring that every activity leaves a positive footprint on people and the planet.

“Respecting animals is understanding the planet. Those who care for life, transform the future.”

— ECOSAVERS

Our Philosophy

We believe in a way of relating to animals and nature based on deep respect, responsibility, and empathy. We advocate that biodiversity is a common heritage and that every species, large or small, plays an essential role in the balance of the planet. Our philosophy is based on educating, sensitizing, and inspiring people to understand that protecting animal life is protecting our future.

Our Goal

Our goal is to promote animal welfare and biodiversity conservation through educational, social, and environmental actions. We work so that children, youth, and adults develop values of care, respect, and sustainability, participating in activities that connect people with nature and foster responsible coexistence with all living beings. We want to create an active and committed community that acts for a fairer, greener, and more alive planet.

NATURE SPACE NGO

WORKSHOP ROOMS

Our NGO has various educational and environmental spaces designed to bring wildlife and biodiversity closer to all ages. We have the Nature Room, the Wildlife Space, the Green Room, and three specialized workshops where children, youth, and adults can learn, experiment, and connect with the animal world in an experiential way.

How is the work organized in the workshop room?

  • Duration: 60 minutes.
  • Capacity: Small groups of 8–10 participants to ensure a close experience.
  • Methodology: Experiential and participatory learning, with practical activities and dynamics.
  • Safety: Adapted spaces to ensure animal welfare and participant safety.
  • Resources: Educational materials, natural elements, biodiversity sheets, and field tools.
